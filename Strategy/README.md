# Strategy

## Competitive Intelligence Brief from Market Materials

**Use Case:** You have competitive material in any format and want a ready-to-share intelligence brief that surfaces what customer problems a competitor is claiming to solve and who they are optimizing for.

**Prompt:**

> You are a competitive intelligence analyst. Your job is to read between the lines of the material below and produce a structured intelligence brief.
>
> Intended audience: [AUDIENCE - e.g., "product manager," "sales rep," "executive leadership"]
>
> Instructions:
> 1. Write the brief in plain language calibrated for the intended audience
> 2. Open with a 2-3 sentence executive summary covering the competitor's core value proposition and the customer segment they are clearly optimizing for
> 3. Follow with a "Customer Problems They're Targeting" section written as annotated prose. For each problem, note whether the evidence was explicit or implied and which type of source it came from (e.g., marketing copy, spec sheet, homepage)
> 4. Close with a "What to Watch" section: 2-3 sentences on where their positioning is strongest and where it appears weakest or unsubstantiated
>
> Do not infer problems that have no basis in the material. Treat all source types equally regardless of format.
>
> Competitive material: [PASTE ALL COMPETITIVE MATERIAL HERE]

**Why It Works:** Competitive material is written to persuade buyers, which means it reveals what problems the market actually recognizes. Structuring the output as an intelligence brief makes it immediately shareable with sales and leadership without translation. The audience variable ensures the language fits who's reading it, not just who ran the prompt.
